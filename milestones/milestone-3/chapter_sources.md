# Earth Story: Chapter Sources & Fact Check

For each chapter card this lists its **claim** (the card body text), the **sources**, and
an **exact citation** quoted word for word from a source that backs the wording. All card
numbers are computed from `docs/data/emdat_clean.csv` ([EM-DAT](https://www.emdat.be/),
CRED / UCLouvain, 1975 to 2025); the external sources below corroborate the events, dates
and framing.

---

## 1. Prologue: *Half a Century of Disasters* (1975)

**Claim.** "Between 1975 and 2025, EM-DAT recorded 15,869 natural disasters across 230
countries. They killed 3,393,822 people and affected billions more."

**Sources.** [EM-DAT](https://www.emdat.be/) (via `emdat_clean.csv`).

**Exact citation.** Dataset totals, computed directly from the cleaned EM-DAT export
(15,869 rows, 230 ISO countries, 3,393,822 summed deaths). These are exact for this
database by construction, not an external estimate.

---

## 2. *Tangshan, 1976* (China earthquake)

**Claim.** "In the early hours of 28 July 1976, an earthquake levelled the Chinese
industrial city of Tangshan. It remains the deadliest single disaster in the modern
record."

**Sources.** [1976 Tangshan earthquake (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1976_Tangshan_earthquake) · [Tangshan earthquake of 1976 (Britannica)](https://www.britannica.com/event/Tangshan-earthquake-of-1976)

**Exact citation.**
> "The 1976 Tangshan earthquake was a Mw 7.6 earthquake that hit the region around Tangshan, Hebei, China, at 19:42:55 UTC on 27 July (03:42:55, 28 July local time)."
> "making it the deadliest earthquake in recorded history (excluding the famine deaths from the 1556 Shaanxi earthquake)" (*Wikipedia*)

---

## 3. *Drought across Ethiopia and Sudan* (1983 to 1985)

**Claim.** "Between 1983 and 1985, drought triggered famine across Ethiopia and Sudan. War
and government failures then turned the shortage into mass starvation that built slowly
over two years."

**Sources.** *Ethiopia:* [1983 to 1985 famine in Ethiopia (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1983%E2%80%931985_famine_in_Ethiopia) · [Ethiopia 1983 to 1985, Historical Human Rights Atlas (IEG Mainz)](https://hhr-atlas.ieg-mainz.de/articles/sasson-ethiopia) · [1980s Ethiopia famine (World Vision)](https://www.worldvision.org/disaster-relief-news-stories/1980s-ethiopia-famine-facts). *Sudan:* [Alexander de Waal, "Is Climate Change the Culprit for Darfur?" (African Arguments)](https://africanarguments.org/2007/06/is-climate-change-the-culprit-for-darfur/) · [de Waal, *Famine that Kills: Darfur, Sudan, 1984 to 1985* (Oxford, 1989), Internet Archive](https://archive.org/details/faminethatkillsd0000dewa)

**Exact citation.**
> "A widespread famine affected Ethiopia from 1983 to 1985."
> "the Mengistu Haile Mariam-led military dictatorship (Derg) used this 1983 to 1985 famine in Ethiopia as government military policy by restricting food supplies"
> "more than half its mortality could be attributed to 'human rights abuses causing the famine to come earlier, strike harder and extend further than would otherwise have been the case'." (*Wikipedia*)
>
> "The proximate cause of the 1984-85 famine was an exceptionally severe two-year drought."
> "the immediate cause of the famine was drought compounded by the government's denial that a problem existed." (*Alexander de Waal, African Arguments*)

## 4. *From Dozens to Hundreds a Year* (trend, 1980 to 2009)

**Claim.** "Through the 1980s, 1990s and 2000s the number of reported disasters climbed
steeply, peaking around 2005 to 2010. Better monitoring explains part of it, but most of
the growth is in climate-related hazards: storms, floods and droughts."

**Sources.** [WMO: Weather-related disasters increased over the past 50 years](https://wmo.int/media/news/weather-related-disasters-increase-over-past-50-years-causing-more-damage-fewer-deaths) · [Our World in Data: Is the number of disasters increasing?](https://ourworldindata.org/disaster-database-limitations) · EM-DAT subgroup spans (`emdat_clean.csv`).

**Exact citation.**
> "The number of disasters has increased by a factor of five over the 50-year period, driven by climate change, more extreme weather and improved reporting." (*WMO*)

## 5. *Cyclone Gorky*, Bangladesh (1991)

**Claim.** "On the night of 29 April 1991, a tropical cyclone drove a six-metre storm
surge into the low-lying coast of Bangladesh, a country that would become a symbol of
climate vulnerability."

**Sources.** [1991 Bangladesh cyclone (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Bangladesh_cyclone) · [Bangladesh cyclone of 1991 (Britannica)](https://www.britannica.com/event/Bangladesh-cyclone-of-1991)

**Exact citation.**
> "At 19:00 UTC on April 29, the cyclone made landfall about 55 km (35 mi) south of Chittagong in southeastern Bangladesh"
> "the cyclone produced a 6.1 m (20 ft) storm surge that inundated the coastline" (*Wikipedia*)

## 6. *The Indian Ocean Tsunami* (2004)

**Claim.** "On 26 December 2004 a magnitude-9.1 earthquake off Sumatra sent waves across
an entire ocean, killing people in fourteen countries on the same morning."

**Sources.** [2004 Indian Ocean earthquake and tsunami (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004_Indian_Ocean_earthquake_and_tsunami) · [Indian Ocean tsunami of 2004 (Britannica)](https://www.britannica.com/event/Indian-Ocean-tsunami-of-2004)

**Exact citation.**
> "On 26 December 2004, at 07:58:53 local time (UTC+7), a Mw 9.2-9.3 earthquake struck with its epicenter off the west coast of Aceh, in northern Sumatra, Indonesia."
> "which devastated communities along the surrounding coasts of the Indian Ocean, killing an estimated 227,898 people in 14 countries, especially in Aceh in Indonesia, Sri Lanka, Tamil Nadu in India, and Khao Lak in Thailand." (*Wikipedia*)

*(The card uses the USGS magnitude of 9.1, within the 9.1 to 9.3 range; Wikipedia's lead states 9.2 to 9.3.)*

## 7. *The Kashmir Earthquake*, Pakistan (2005)

**Claim.** "Less than a year later, on 8 October 2005, a quake in the mountainous Kashmir
region of Pakistan destroyed villages that rescue teams could barely reach before winter
set in."

**Sources.** [2005 Kashmir earthquake (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_Kashmir_earthquake) · [The Kashmir earthquake of October 8, 2005 (ReliefWeb)](https://reliefweb.int/report/pakistan/kashmir-earthquake-october-8-2005-impacts-pakistan)

**Exact citation.**
> "An earthquake occurred at 08:50:39 Pakistan Standard Time on 8 October 2005 in Azad Jammu and Kashmir"
> "Limited infrastructure, combined with harsh weather and mountainous terrain, delayed the transport of supplies to remote areas."
> "cold weather increased the death toll for those who survived the earthquake, but were displaced and homeless." (*Wikipedia*)

## 8. *Nargis and Sichuan* (2008)

**Claim.** "2008 struck twice. On 2 May, Cyclone Nargis flooded the Irrawaddy Delta of
Myanmar; on 12 May the Sichuan earthquake devastated central China."

**Sources.** [Cyclone Nargis (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclone_Nargis) · [2008 Sichuan earthquake (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Sichuan_earthquake) · [Myanmar 2008 PDNA (GFDRR)](https://www.gfdrr.org/en/myanmar-2008-pdna-undertaken-after-cyclone-nargis-killed-84537-people)

**Exact citation.**
> "Around 1200 UTC on 2 May, Cyclone Nargis made landfall in the Ayeyarwady Division of Myanmar at peak strength."
> "The Irrawaddy delta was hit extremely hard not only because of the wind and rain but because of the storm surge." (*Wikipedia, Cyclone Nargis*)
>
> "An earthquake occurred in the province of Sichuan, China at 14:28:01 China Standard Time on May 12, 2008." (*Wikipedia, 2008 Sichuan earthquake*)

## 9. *The Haiti Earthquake* (2010)

**Claim.** "On 12 January 2010 a shallow magnitude-7.0 earthquake struck just outside
Port-au-Prince, collapsing the Haitian capital in seconds. With hospitals, ports and
government buildings destroyed, the toll climbed into the hundreds of thousands."

**Sources.** [2010 Haiti earthquake (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_Haiti_earthquake) · [The True Haiti Earthquake Death Toll (Scientific American)](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/the-true-haiti-earthquake-death-toll-is-much-worse-than-early-official-counts/)

**Exact citation.**
> "A catastrophic magnitude 7.0 Mw earthquake struck Haiti at 16:53 local time (21:53 UTC) on Tuesday, 12 January 2010."
> "The epicenter was near the town of Léogâne, Ouest department, approximately 25 kilometres (16 mi) west of Port-au-Prince, Haiti's capital." (*Wikipedia*)

## 10. *The Climate Era* (trend, 2010 to 2024)

**Claim.** "By the 2010s, storms, floods and droughts made up the overwhelming majority of
disasters. Geophysical events grew modestly with better detection; the climate curve grew
much faster."

**Sources.** [WMO: Weather-related disasters increased over the past 50 years](https://wmo.int/media/news/weather-related-disasters-increase-over-past-50-years-causing-more-damage-fewer-deaths) · EM-DAT subgroup spans (`emdat_clean.csv`; Meteorological plus Hydrological plus Climatological vs Geophysical).

**Exact citation.**
> "The number of disasters has increased by a factor of five over the 50-year period, driven by climate change, more extreme weather and improved reporting." (*WMO*)

---

## 11. *The Turkey and Syria Earthquake* (2023)

**Claim.** "On 6 February 2023 a pair of powerful earthquakes ruptured the border region
of Turkey and Syria, the deadliest event of the decade so far."

**Sources.** [2023 Turkey and Syria earthquakes (Wikipedia)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Turkey%E2%80%93Syria_earthquakes) · [Türkiye and Syria earthquake response (United Nations)](https://www.un.org/en/turkiye-syria-earthquake-response)

**Exact citation.**
> "On 6 February 2023, at 04:17:35 TRT (01:17:35 UTC), a moment magnitude (Mw) 7.8 earthquake struck southern and central Turkey and northern and western Syria."
> "It was followed by a Mw 7.7 earthquake, at 13:24:49 TRT (10:24:49 UTC)."
> "the deadliest earthquake or natural disaster in general since the 2010 Haiti earthquake; and the fifth-deadliest earthquake of the 21st century." (*Wikipedia*)

## 12. Epilogue: *Where We Stand* (2025)

**Claim.** "Fifty years on, disasters arrive more often than they once did, and the
climate-related share keeps growing." (Card stats: climate share 1975 to 1984 = 77%,
2015 to 2024 = 88%.)

**Sources.** [WMO: Weather-related disasters increased over the past 50 years](https://wmo.int/media/news/weather-related-disasters-increase-over-past-50-years-causing-more-damage-fewer-deaths) · EM-DAT climate-share spans (`emdat_clean.csv`).

**Exact citation.**
> "The number of disasters has increased by a factor of five over the 50-year period, driven by climate change, more extreme weather and improved reporting." (*WMO*)

The 77% to 88% climate-share figures are computed directly from EM-DAT subgroups for the
first and most recent full decades.
